# Platform Benchmarks

Baseline "what good looks like" by channel. These are starting reference points — replace with your own data after 2-3 cycles.

## Twitter / X

| Metric | Median | Good | Great |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| Impressions per tweet | 200-500 | 1K-5K | 10K+ |
| Engagement rate | 1-2% | 3-5% | 8%+ |
| Link CTR | 0.5-1% | 1-3% | 5%+ |
| Profile visits per tweet | 1-3 | 5-15 | 25+ |
| Follower conversion (visit → follow) | 2-5% | 5-15% | 20%+ |

Notes: Threads outperform singles for engagement. Media tweets outperform text-only for impressions. Link tweets get suppressed in feed.

## Blog / SEO

| Metric | Median | Good | Great |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| Organic CTR (position 1-3) | 3-5% | 5-10% | 15%+ |
| Avg time on page | 30-60s | 1-3 min | 5+ min |
| Bounce rate | 70-80% | 50-70% | <50% |
| Conversion rate (visitor → action) | 1-2% | 2-5% | 5%+ |
| Pages per session | 1.2-1.5 | 1.5-2.5 | 3+ |

Notes: Long-tail keywords have lower volume but higher intent. First-page ranking typically takes 3-6 months for new domains. Content freshness signals matter for competitive queries.

## Email

| Metric | Median | Good | Great |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| Open rate | 15-20% | 20-30% | 35%+ |
| Click rate | 1-3% | 3-5% | 7%+ |
| Click-to-open rate | 8-12% | 12-18% | 20%+ |
| Unsubscribe rate (per send) | 0.1-0.3% | <0.1% | <0.05% |
| Conversion rate | 0.5-1% | 1-3% | 5%+ |

Notes: Segmented sends outperform blasts by 2-3x. Plain text often beats designed HTML for B2B. Subject line is ~80% of open rate. Best send times are audience-specific (test, don't assume).

## Outreach (Cold Email / DM)

| Metric | Median | Good | Great |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| Open rate | 30-40% | 50-60% | 70%+ |
| Reply rate | 1-3% | 5-10% | 15%+ |
| Positive reply rate | 0.5-1% | 2-5% | 8%+ |
| Meeting book rate | 0.2-0.5% | 1-3% | 5%+ |
| Bounce rate (bad emails) | 5-10% | <5% | <2% |

Notes: Personalization is the single biggest lever. Follow-ups generate 50%+ of replies. 3-5 touch sequence is standard. After 4 follow-ups, returns diminish sharply.

## Product Listings (E-commerce / Marketplace)

| Metric | Median | Good | Great |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| Search CTR | 1-3% | 3-7% | 10%+ |
| Listing conversion rate | 2-5% | 5-10% | 15%+ |
| Add-to-cart rate | 5-10% | 10-20% | 25%+ |
| Cart abandonment | 60-80% | 50-60% | <50% |
| Return rate | 10-20% | 5-10% | <5% |

Notes: Main image drives CTR. Price and reviews drive conversion. A/B test titles before images (titles affect search ranking).

## GitHub / Dev Marketing

| Metric | Median | Good | Great |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| README → star rate (visitors) | 1-3% | 5-10% | 15%+ |
| Star → clone rate | 5-10% | 10-20% | 25%+ |
| Clone → active user rate | 1-5% | 5-15% | 20%+ |
| Issue response time | days | <24h | <4h |
| Contributor conversion | <1% | 1-3% | 5%+ |

Notes: First 30 seconds of README determines star. GIF/screenshot in first fold is near-mandatory. "Getting started in <5 min" is table stakes. Launch on HN/Reddit for initial spike, then SEO sustains.

---

These benchmarks decay fast. Update with your own data after each measurement cycle. Your audience, niche, and offer will make these numbers vary significantly.
